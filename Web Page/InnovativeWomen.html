Nancy Lieberman

Basic Facts

  1. Born on July 1, 1958 in Brooklyn, New York
  2. Full Name: Nancy Elizabeth Lieberman
  3. Height: 5ft. 10in.
  4. Position: point guard
  5. Playing Career: 1980-2008
  6. Coaching Career: 1998-present
  7. High School: Far Rockaway
  8. College:Old Dominion

Biography

Nancy Lieberman is from Brooklyn, New York. For college, Nancy entered Old Dominion University in 1976, and while she was attending this school she led her team to achieve great things. Nancy was a tough, game smart, and quick point guard that was best known for her defense and her accurate shooting touch.

Nancy also continued her basketball career after college at the international level. Nancy was apart of Team USA for a long time, and was on the olympic team for the 1976 Olympics and the 1980 Olympics.

Lieberman participated in many of the first professional leagues for both men and women.

Lieberman than became a coach at Detroit Shock, a WNBA team, for three years. After this she became a women's basketball analyst for ESPN. Lieberman also worked in the NBA with a team called the Texas Legends who were in the NBA development league, which is connected with the Dallas Mavericks. Then later on Lieberman was hired to become the Sacramento Kings assistant coach, which made her the second female NBA assistant coach in history.

Accomplishments

  • Three Time Kodak All American
  • Led Old Dominion to win back-to-back AIAW National Championships
  • The only two time winner of the Wade Trophy
  • Received the Broderick Cup as the nation's top female athlete in 1980
  • Inducted into the Basketball Hall of Fame in 1996
  • Was apart of the Olympic team, and won a silver medal
